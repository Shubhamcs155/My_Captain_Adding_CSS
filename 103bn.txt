<div class="a"><br>***ENGINEERING PHYSICS***<br><br></div>
<hr><br><br>
<div class="b">
Unit 1: Particle Nature of Waves (07 Hrs.)<br>
Planck’s quantum theory of light, Explanation of laws of photoelectric emission in terms of quantum
nature of light. Compton effect again in terms of quantum nature of waves.
</div>
<div class="c">Unit 2: Wave Nature of Particles (07 Hrs.)<br>
De Broglie’s concept of matter waves, Davisson –Germer experiment, G. P. Thomson’s experiment.
</div>
<div class="d">Unit 3: Wave Optics (08 Hrs.)<br>
Huygens’s principle, superposition of waves and interference of light, Young’s double slit experiment,<br>
Newton’s rings, Michelson interferometer, Mach-Zehnder interferometer.<br>
Fraunhofer diffraction from a single slit and a circular aperture, the Rayleigh criterion for limit of<br>
resolution and its application to vision; Diffraction gratings and their resolving power.</div>

<div class="e">Unit 4:Quantum Mechanics (08 Hrs)<br>Fundamental difference between Newtonian mechanics and quantum mechanics, Uncertainty principle,<br>
Derivation of wave equation propagating along a stretched string and its solution. The wave function.<br>
requirements that must fulfill. Schrodinger equation (time dependent and steady-state form in one and<br>
three dimensions) expectation values, operators, Eigen values, Eigen functions. Particle in a one<br>
dimension rigid box i) energy quantization, ii) Wave function & iii) momentum quantization.</div>


<div class="f">Unit 5: Introduction to Molecular Bonding (07 Hrs.)<br>
Particle in double delta-function potential, Molecules (hydrogen molecule, valence bond and molecular<br>
orbitals picture), singlet/triplet states, chemical bonding, hybridization.</div>


<div class="g">Unit 6: Introduction to Solids (08 Hrs.)<br>
Free electron theory of metals, Fermi level, density of states, Application to white dwarfs and neutron<br>
stars, Bloch’s theorem for particles in a periodic potential, Kronig-Penney model and origin of energy<br>
bands, Numerical solution for energy in one-dimensional periodic lattice by mixing plane waves.
</div>
