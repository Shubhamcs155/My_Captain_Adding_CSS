<div class="a"><br>
***BASIC ELECTRICAL ENGINEERING***<br><br></div><br>
<hr><br><br>
<div class="b">
Unit 1: DC Circuits (08 Hrs.)<br>
Concepts of e.m.f, Potential difference & current, Battery, Energy Sources: Ideal and practical voltage<br>
and current sources, Independent and dependent sources, Ohm's law, Kirchhoff’s laws, Simplification of<br>
networks using series-parallel combinations and star-delta transformations, Current and Voltage division<br>
rule, Mesh Analysis, Nodal Analysis, Superposition Theorem, Thevenin's Theorem, Norton's Theorem,<br>
Maximum power transfer theorem, Time domain analysis of RL & RC circuits.<br>
</div>
<div class="c">
Unit 2: AC Fundamentals (08 Hrs.)<br>
Sinusoidal voltages and currents, Basic definitions of AC fundamentals. Phase difference, lagging,<br>
leading and in phase quantities and phasor representation. Study of A.C. circuit consisting of pure R, L &<br>
C. Single phase A.C. circuits: Study of series and parallel R-L, R-C, R-L-C circuits, resonance, Concept<br>
of impedance, Admittance in case of above combinations, Waveform and relevant voltage-current phasor<br>
diagrams, Concept of active, Reactive, Apparent, Complex power and power factor. Three phase balanced<br>
circuits, Voltage and current relations in star and delta connections.
</div>
<div class="d">
Unit 3: Electromagnetism (06 Hrs.)<br>
Magnetic effect of electric current, Cross and dot conventions, Right hand thumb rule, Force on current<br>
carrying conductors placed in magnetic field, Fleming's left hand rule, Fleming's right hand rule.<br>
Faraday’s laws of electromagnetic induction, Statically and dynamically induced e.m.f., Self and mutual<br>
inductance, Coefficient of coupling, energy stored in magnetic field.</div>
<div class="e">Unit 4: Transformers (08 Hrs.)<br>
Basic definitions of magnetic circuits, Comparison of electrical and magnetic circuit, B-H curve<br>
Transformer: Theory of operation, Ideal and practical transformer, Open circuit test. Short-circuit test,<br>
Efficiency and Voltage Regulation, Auto transformers.</div>
 <div class="f">
Unit 5: Rotating Electrical Machines (08 Hrs.)<br>
DC Machines: Construction, Working principles, operation, Types & equivalent circuit of DC Generator,<br>
DC Motor. AC Machines: Construction, Working principles, Operation, Types & equivalent circuit of 3-<br>
phase induction motor, single phase induction motor, Alternators (AC Generators).</div>
<div class="g">  
Unit 6: Electrical Installation (06 Hrs.)<br>
Components of LT Switchgear: Switch Fuse Unit (SFU), Miniature Circuit Breaker (MCB), Earth<br>
Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB), Earthing. Types of Batteries, Important Characteristics for Batteries,<br>
Power factor improvement.</div><br><br>
