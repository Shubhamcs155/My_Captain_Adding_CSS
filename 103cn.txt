<div class="a"><br>***MATHEMATICS-1***<br><br></div>
<hr><br><br>
<div class="b">
Unit 1: Differential Calculus (07 Hrs.)<br>
Review of limits, continuity, differentiability, linearization and differentials. maxima and minima, Mean<br>
value theorem, concavity. Taylor’s theorem.
</div>
<div class="c">
Unit 2: Integral Calculus (08 Hrs.)<br>
Riemann integrals, fundamental theorem of calculus, improper integrals, beta and gamma functions and<br>
their properties, applications to areas and volumes, transcendental functions, indeterminate forms and<br>
L’Hospital’s rule.</div>
<div class="d">
Unit 3: Sequence and Series (09 Hrs.)<br>
Convergence of sequences and series, tests for convergence, power series, Taylor's series and its<br>
convergence, series for exponential, trigonometric and logarithmic functions.</div>
<div class="e">
Unit 4: Matrices (06 Hrs.)<br>
Matrices and matrix operations (review), rank of a matrix, determinants and rank of a matrix in terms of<br>
determinants, geometry of linear equations, systems of linear equations, Gauss elimination, Gauss-Jordan<br>
elimination.</div>
<div class="f">
Unit 5: Vector Spaces (08 Hrs.)<br>
Real vector spaces, subspaces, linear independence, coordinates, basis, dimension, linear transformations<br>
(maps), range and kernel of a linear map, rank and nullity, inverse of linear transformation, rank-nullity<br>
theorem, composition of liner maps, matrix associated with linear map.</div>
<div class="g">
Unit 6: Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors (07 Hrs.)<br>
Eigenvalues and eigenvectors, characteristic polynomials, Cayley-Hamilton theorem, eigenvalues of<br>
special matrices (orthogonal, unitary, Hermitian, symmetric, skew-symmetric, normal), algebraic and<br>
geometric multiplicity, diagonalization.</div>
